DELIRIUM Mnemonic:
D - Drugs (benzos, anticholinergics)
E - Electrolytes (Na, Ca)
L - Low O2 (check pulse ox)
I - Infection (UTI, pneumonia)
R - Retention (bladder/stool)
I - Intracranial (stroke, subdural)
U - Uremia
M - Myocardial (MI, CHF)

POLYPHARMACY KILLERS רב-תרופתיות קטלנית

  • Benzos → Falls (2.7x risk)
  • Anticholinergics → Confusion
  • NSAIDs → Renal failure
  • PPIs >8 weeks → C.diff, fractures

QUICK ASSESSMENTS

MINI-COG (3 min)

  1. Remember 3 words
  2. Draw clock (11:10)
  3. Recall 3 words

Score: 0-2 recall + abnormal clock = POSITIVE

CAM (Confusion Assessment)

Need: (1) Acute + (2) Inattention
AND: (3) Disorganized OR (4) Altered LOC

ADLs (Can't do = needs help)

  • Bathing
  • Dressing
  • Toileting
  • Transferring
  • Continence
  • Feeding

MORSE Fall Scale

  • History of falls: 25 points
  • Secondary diagnosis: 15 points
  • Ambulatory aid: 15-30 points
  • IV/Heparin lock: 20 points
  • Gait impaired: 10-20 points
  • Mental status: 15 points

>45 = HIGH RISK

🚩 NEVER MISS THESE

NEW CONFUSION
= DELIRIUM until proven otherwise
→ Full workup NOW
NEW INCONTINENCE
= Cord compression until proven otherwise
→ Check rectal tone, urgent MRI
FAILURE TO THRIVE
= Depression vs Cancer
→ PHQ-9, CBC, CMP, consider CT
REPEATED FALLS
= Parkinson's, NPH, subdural
→ Neuro exam, head CT, gait assessment
WEIGHT LOSS >5%
= Cancer, depression, dementia
→ Full review, depression screen, CT

ATTENDING WILL ASK:

  • "Did you check orthostatics?" (>20/10 drop)
  • "What's the med count?" (>4 = problem)
  • "Did you do a CAM?" (know it cold!)
  • "What's the eGFR?" (Cr 1.0 in 80yo = ~50)
  • "Is this delirium or dementia?" (know difference!)
